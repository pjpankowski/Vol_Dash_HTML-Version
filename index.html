ETF Arbitrage & Market Microstructure

Real-time ETF vs NAV pricing with creation/redemption flows and order flow toxicity analysis

üìö How ETF Creation/Redemption Works

Creation Process: Authorized Participants (APs) deliver a basket of underlying securities to the ETF issuer and receive ETF shares in return (typically 50,000 share blocks called "creation units").

Redemption Process: APs return ETF shares to the issuer and receive the underlying basket. This mechanism keeps ETF prices aligned with NAV.

Arbitrage Opportunity: When ETF price > NAV (premium), APs create shares and sell them, pocketing the spread. When ETF price < NAV (discount), APs buy cheap ETF shares and redeem for underlying basket.

Impact on Trading: Large creation/redemption flows affect underlying basket liquidity and can signal directional moves. High VPIN during these flows indicates informed trading.

üìö Real Trading Examples

IC (Information Coefficient): If your model predicts tomorrow's returns with IC=0.10, you have 10% correlation between predictions and actual outcomes. Elite funds achieve IC of 0.05-0.15.

Breadth: A daily vol strategy has breadth=252 (trading days). An intraday strategy with 4 rebalances/day has breadth=1,008. More independent bets compound skill.

TC (Transfer Coefficient): Measures slippage between optimal and actual portfolio due to constraints. TC=1.0 means perfect implementation. Real-world TC typically 0.7-0.9 due to position limits, costs, liquidity.

Expected Alpha: Calculated as IR √ó Active Risk. If IR=2.0 and you run 8% active risk, expect 16% annual alpha.
